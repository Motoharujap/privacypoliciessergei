Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how ThornScan: Garden Protector ("the app", "we", "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use our Android application, available on the Google Play Store.

By using ThornScan: Garden Protector, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the app.

We do not require you to create an account, and we do not directly collect your name, email address, or other personal registration details.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Photos You Take

When you photograph a plant or weed using the app, that photo is transmitted to third-party AI and plant identification services (Google Gemini API and Kindwise API) for analysis. Photos are used solely for plant identification and are not stored by us on our own servers.

2.2 Device Identifiers

Certain third-party services (advertising and analytics) collect device-level identifiers such as your Android Advertising ID (AAID). These are used for ad delivery and anonymous usage analytics. We do not directly access or store these identifiers ourselves.

2.3 Purchase and Subscription Data

If you purchase a premium subscription, our subscription management provider (RevenueCat) processes and stores your purchase history, subscription status, and associated device identifiers. Payment card details are handled by Google Play, not by us.

2.4 App Usage Events

Firebase Analytics collects anonymous data about how you interact with the app — which features you use, how often you open it, and which screens you visit. This data does not identify you personally.

2.5 Crash Reports

Firebase Crashlytics automatically collects crash reports containing technical information about the error, device model, OS version, and app version. Crash reports do not include your photos or personal identity information.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Plant identification: Photos are sent to Google Gemini API and Kindwise API to identify weeds, invasive species, toxic plants, and growth suppressors.
  • App improvement: Anonymous usage analytics and crash data help us identify bugs and improve the app.
  • Advertising: Device identifiers are used by Google AdMob to display advertisements. These may be personalized or non-personalized depending on your consent.
  • Subscription management: Purchase and subscription data is used to unlock premium features.
  • Gamification features: Audit records, badges, and task progress are stored locally on your device and are not transmitted to our servers.

4. Third-Party Services

4.1 Google AdMob — Advertising

AdMob displays advertisements within the app. It may collect your Android Advertising ID, IP address, and ad interaction data. Where required by law (EU/EEA), the app presents a consent prompt before displaying personalized ads. If you decline, non-personalized ads will be shown. You can opt out at any time via device Settings → Google → Ads.

4.2 RevenueCat — In-App Purchases & Subscriptions

RevenueCat manages in-app purchase verification and subscription status. It receives your purchase history, subscription entitlement, and a device-level identifier. Payment card information is handled exclusively by Google Play.

RevenueCat Privacy Policy ↗

4.3 Google Gemini API — AI Plant Analysis

When you photograph a plant, the image is sent to Google's Gemini API for AI-powered identification. Google processes this photo on their servers to return an identification result.

4.4 Kindwise API — Plant Identification

Photos may also be sent to the Kindwise plant identification API for species identification. Kindwise processes images on their servers to return results.

Kindwise Privacy Policy ↗

4.5 Firebase Analytics & Crashlytics

Firebase Analytics collects anonymized usage events. Firebase Crashlytics collects crash logs and device diagnostics. Both are operated by Google. Data is aggregated and does not personally identify you.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. Data is shared with third parties only as described in Section 4, when required by applicable law, or in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets (in which case you will be notified via an updated Privacy Policy).

6. Data Retention

  • Photos: Transmitted to Gemini and Kindwise for analysis; not retained on our servers. Retention by those providers is governed by their own policies.
  • Analytics and crash data: Retained by Firebase, typically up to 14 months.
  • Subscription data: Retained by RevenueCat as long as necessary for subscription management.
  • Local app data: Garden audits, tasks, and badges are stored on your device and deleted when you uninstall the app.
